§ Approach

All three courses run on the same wager: AI belongs in the methods classroom as a cognitive partner, not as a shortcut. Students work with Claude on real analytical tasks, then show, in no-AI checkpoints, process logs, and closed-book syntheses, that the thinking is still theirs. The point is not to protect the curriculum from AI or to hand it over. It is to teach students to reach further with it than either could alone, and to know the difference.
