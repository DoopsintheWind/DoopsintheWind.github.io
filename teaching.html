Teaching

I teach at the Department of Political Science, Nazarbayev University. My courses focus on how people organize, resist, and make sense of power -- and on the methods we use to study that.

Current courses (Spring 2026)

PLS 345: Social Movements

Why do people mobilize? When does collective action succeed, fail, or transform? This course examines social movements from civil rights to climate activism, with attention to how movements work in non-democratic and hybrid regime contexts.

PLS 511: Qualitative Methods

A graduate seminar on interpretive and qualitative research design. Students learn to work with interviews, ethnography, and case studies -- and to think critically about what counts as evidence and how knowledge is produced.
