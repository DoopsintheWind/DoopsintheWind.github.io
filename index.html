Hi, I'm Dina.

Political scientist at Nazarbayev University, Astana

I study how states respond to crisis and vulnerability -- from vaccination campaigns to domestic violence shelters to the politics of public trust. My work sits at the intersection of interpretive social science, qualitative methods, and the communities that policy often overlooks.

More recently, I've been exploring what happens when AI enters qualitative research not as a tool but as a collaborator. I'm interested in what that changes about how we see patterns, build knowledge, and think about authorship.

Before academia, I lived several other lives. I bring that with me.
