§ Synopsis

I study how people interpret contested things: what they take to be true, who they trust and why, and how they make sense of the choices they're given. I'm an Assistant Professor in Political Science at Nazarbayev University in Astana, Kazakhstan, working across qualitative research on gender-based violence and attitudes towards sexual and gender minorities, survey experiment on securitisation framing and public support for state violence, and vaccination attitudes.

I'm also building a practice around AI-augmented social science - AI as a genuine cognitive partner in research and teaching. The field is wide open, and most of the interesting questions haven't been asked yet.
