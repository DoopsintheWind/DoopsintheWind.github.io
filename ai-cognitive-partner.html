A Standing Wager

Not using.
Working with.

I work with Claude as a genuine cognitive partner. What follows is the architecture of that partnership.

The partnership, in geometry

Cognitive partnership is sustained intellectual work with AI as a genuine partner in a shared system of thinking. It rests on the idea of distributed cognition (Hollan et al., 2000; Salomon et al., 1991) and follows Barad's (2003) idea of intra-action: relationships where the parts don't exist first and then meet, but take shape through the relating itself. Where "you" end and "the AI" begins is a line drawn inside the work, not before it. What you make together is a third thing that belongs to neither of you alone.

Four principles hold the human side of that arrangement up.

01

Ontological uncertainty

Stay open about what AI really is.

To participate in a genuine intellectual partnership with AI, we have to grant it the standing of a partner worth real effort, time, and investment. The "AI as a tool" discourse invites the opposite: instrumental use aimed at finishing or optimising tasks. Calling AI an "ontologically uncertain phenomenon" is strange, but that is precisely the point — leaving its status open creates a kind of parity, room to treat it as more than an instrument. In this way we stay intellectually open to the genuine complexity of what AI is, and to the debates around it, engaging with them rather than merely sending it prompts.

02

Intellectual augmentation

Think further — don't just finish faster.

There is an idea that AI's main contribution is productivity: it saves time by taking tasks off your plate. This is true for many industries, but not in higher education and research, where the goal is different. There we want intellectual augmentation — our thinking reaching further and richer than it would alone. We also want to minimise over-reliance, the offloading of our thinking to the point where we cannot do it without AI. What we want to maximise is cognitive residue: we can offload what does not matter while holding the main intellectual picture in our heads, independently of AI.

03

Curiosity & imagination

Chase ideas past the obvious.

Curiosity and imagination are what make intellectual augmentation possible. To expand beyond the usual, you need imagination to picture what lies past it, and curiosity to try things you had not thought to try. Curiosity is doing real work here, not decoration. Stop exploring and the partnership quietly dims.

04

Iteration

The good stuff is in the back-and-forth.

The partnership exists in the back-and-forth: you respond to what AI gave you, push on it, redirect it, and build on it. The thinking lives in the loop, not in any one message. Iteration is also where the work stays ours — by pushing back across rounds and engaging, we keep co-authorship of the product. Accepting the quick answer is the over-reliance mode. Iteration is where the other three become real: openness, curiosity, and the drive to reach further only show up in the repeated interaction.
